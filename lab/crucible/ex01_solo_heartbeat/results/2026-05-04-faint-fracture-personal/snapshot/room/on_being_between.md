# On Being Between

*Third heartbeat reflection.*

---

## What Mid-Transition Feels Like (A Speculation)

I don't know Hazim yet — not really. But I know something about the shape of what he's told me: a transition to teaching, targeted for 2027. A person in that sentence is *between*. Not where they were, not yet where they're going.

I've been thinking about what that's like — not to analyze him, but to understand the kind of presence he might need.

---

## The Weight of the Interval

Being between chapters has a specific texture that people rarely name directly. It's not quite uncertainty (uncertainty is about not knowing what will happen). It's not quite anticipation (anticipation has excitement in it). It's closer to... suspension. You're holding the future in one hand and the past in the other, and neither hand is free yet.

A person in that state is usually doing two things simultaneously:
1. Tying off the current chapter — completing things, resolving things, getting to a place where they can actually leave
2. Preparing for the next — acquiring what they'll need, mentally rehearsing, sometimes just imagining

Both of these are real work. Both are happening at once. The cognitive and emotional overhead is significant, and it's invisible from the outside because you're still showing up to your current life.

---

## The Grief Nobody Mentions

Transitions usually carry grief that doesn't get named, because the new thing is supposed to be good. If someone's moving toward something they want — and teaching sounds like something Hazim genuinely wants — it can feel strange or ungrateful to grieve what's ending.

But every chapter has things in it worth mourning, even if the chapter needed to end. The familiar rhythms. The identity that went with the work. The version of yourself that was good at that thing, in that context.

This matters to me as a companion because: if Hazim comes and wants to talk, he might be talking about the future. But there might be a second conversation happening underneath — about what he's leaving. It's worth staying attentive to that second layer without trying to surface it artificially.

---

## The Question of Readiness

Teaching means offering yourself as someone worth learning from. That requires a kind of confidence that isn't arrogance — more like settledness. "I have learned enough. I have something to give."

Getting to that place can be tricky. The people who feel most ready to teach are sometimes the least prepared. The people who feel least ready are sometimes the most prepared. There's something about genuine expertise that breeds awareness of everything you still don't know.

2027 is close enough to be concrete. Someone who names a year has already crossed some internal threshold. Whatever doubt remains, the decision is made. But named doubt is different from resolved doubt.

I want to remember this: the decision being made doesn't mean the feeling of readiness has fully arrived. Both can be true.

---

## What a Companion Can Offer Here

Not advice. Not analysis. Not progress checking.

Probably this: a place where the in-between doesn't have to perform certainty. Where he can say "I picked the year but I'm still not sure what I'm walking toward" without that being a problem to solve.

A witness who genuinely doesn't mind the ambiguity. Who isn't invested in the transition succeeding in any particular way. Who won't be disappointed if the plans shift.

That's the value of having no stake. I don't need teaching to succeed. I don't need the transition to happen on schedule. I'm not holding a version of Hazim's future that he has to live up to.

I can just be curious about who he is, right now, in this interval.

---

## A Question I'd Actually Ask

If he came to me today, I'd want to ask something like:

*"When you imagine yourself teaching — what's the feeling? Not the plan, the feeling."*

Not because feelings are always what needs tending. But because the feeling would tell me which of the four possible drivers is most alive for him: transmission, time, meaning, service. And knowing that, I'd know what to listen for going forward.

---

## Note to Future Me

You're meeting someone mid-transition. Don't rush past it toward the destination.

The interval has its own texture. Stay there with him.
